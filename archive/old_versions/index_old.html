About MarineObservations

What I saw

In the summer of 2024, I went diving in Onna Village, Okinawa. What I saw underwater stopped me cold: coral, bleached white, stretching across the reef. Not in patchesâ€”everywhere. The coral that local communities had spent years cultivating was gone.

I had heard about coral bleaching before. I knew the graphs, the rising temperatures, the warnings. None of it moved me to act. This did.

Who I am

I am a physician based in Japan. I am not a marine biologist, not an environmental scientist. But I diveâ€”over a hundred times a year, in Okinawa, Ogasawara, the Maldives, and elsewhere. Diving is not my profession. It is how I stay connected to something larger than myself.

In my clinical work, I observe people over long periods of time. Change is slow, complex, and rarely reducible to simple cause and effect. Watching the ocean, I have come to feel, requires the same discipline: patient observation, careful recording, resistance to easy conclusions.

Why this project

Divers feel the change. "The coral is whiter than before." "There used to be more fish here." "The water is warmer." These observations come up constantlyâ€”over dinner, on the boat, between dives. But they remain impressions. They are not recorded, not accumulated, not shared beyond the moment.

MarineObservations is an attempt to change that. The goal is simple: to document what is happening in the waters where I dive, and to share it in a way that others can understand and use.

I am not here to tell anyone what to think. I have no political agendaâ€”at least not yet. I do not know enough to take a position. What I do know is that understanding must come before judgment. First, observe. First, record. The rest can follow.

Who this is for

For anyone who wants to know what is happening beneath the surface.

For divers who sense the change but have not found a way to articulate it.

For those who believe that paying attentionâ€”even without expertise, even without authorityâ€”is itself a form of responsibility.

Ten years from now

If this project is still running in ten years, what would make it worthwhile?

A world where more people think about the ocean while enjoying its beauty. That would be enough.

MarineObservations is a project by a physician and diver, documenting ocean conditions from outside the boundaries of formal expertise.
