When ocean temperatures rise above the normal summer maximum (typically >30Â°C sustained), corals become stressed and expel these algae, revealing their white calcium carbonate skeleton.

The Great Barrier Reef has experienced 8 mass bleaching events since 1998 (1998, 2002, 2016, 2017, 2020, 2022, 2024, 2025). Since coral recovery typically requires 10-15 years, the current frequency doesn't allow sufficient recovery time.
